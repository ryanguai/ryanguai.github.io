School of Christian Leadership 2023

Hebrews 11:1 - “Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen”.

Struggling with Cynicism

I came into SOCL23 struggling with an attitude of cynicism towards God. While ministering to His people, I doubted if my service was efficacious. While spending time in prayer, I doubted if He was there or even cared about me. In the various struggles that I had, I doubted if God could bring new life where death once was, instead resorting to grasping control and self-reliance.

A Revelation of God's Presence

On the first day of the school, Father’s homily on looking at my life “through heaven’s eyes” really struck me. In prayer, I was invited to look back on all these memories and recollect where the Lord had been moving. Somehow, with God’s grace, I was able to see that the Lord had indeed been very near to me in every single moment, especially when I felt most alone. He assured me that He would walk with me always and that I would never be forsaken or abandoned by Him.

Putting God at the Center

Pope Benedict once said that "Being Christian is not the result of an ethical choice or a lofty idea, but the encounter with an event, a person, which gives life a new horizon and a decisive direction." During the school, I also had time to ponder on what I desire out of this life. I can now say with conviction that I want to put God at the center of all that I do. I want to be able to say that “I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith”. May this be the cry of my heart every time I am tempted to stray away from you Lord.

Faith Beyond Feelings

he biggest lesson for me this SOCL is that faith is more than my feelings. Even when I do not feel His presence, I claim the truth that He is for me and not against me. This faith is not a passive assent to the Church’s teachings, but alove for my Father in Heaven who wills every good for me. I no longer want to be controlled by my fears, but to walk in the security that You are in control.

Thank you Jesus for the gift of faith. Thank you for giving your life for me. I am privileged and blessed to be able to know and love You.
